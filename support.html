Getting Started

  1. Grant Permissions: Allow camera and photo library access when prompted
  2. Position Yourself: Stand in good lighting facing the camera
  3. Wait for Detection: The skeleton overlay will appear when pose is detected
  4. Choose Style: Tap "STYLE" to select your preferred skeleton visualization
  5. Start Recording: Tap the record button to capture your movement with overlays

System Requirements

  • iOS Version: iOS 18.0 or later (required)
  • Device: iPhone or iPad with camera
  • Optimal Performance: iPhone 12 or newer recommended
  • Storage: Sufficient space in Photos library for video export

Troubleshooting

Skeleton Not Appearing

  • Ensure you're using iOS 18.0 or later
  • Check that camera permission is granted in Settings > Privacy & Security > Camera
  • Improve lighting conditions
  • Stand fully in frame with clear view of your body
  • Try different clothing with good contrast against background

App Won't Open/Crashes

  • Restart the app by force-closing and reopening
  • Restart your device
  • Check for iOS updates in Settings > General > Software Update
  • Ensure your device meets the iOS 18.0 requirement

Camera Permission Issues

  • Go to Settings > Privacy & Security > Camera > Traeceet
  • Toggle the permission off and back on
  • Restart the app after changing permissions

Can't Save Videos

  • Check Photos permission in Settings > Privacy & Security > Photos > Traeceet
  • Ensure you have sufficient storage space
  • Try recording a shorter video first

Hand Tracking Not Working

  • Hand tracking is optional - toggle it on in the side controls or style settings
  • Ensure good lighting on your hands
  • Keep hands clearly visible and unobstructed
  • Hand detection works best at close to medium range

Features Guide

Skeleton Styles

  • Classic Minimal: Clean white lines for professional analysis
  • Neon Pulse: Vibrant colors with pulsing animation
  • Artistic Sketch: Black sketch-style lines for artistic expression

Advanced Features

  • Movement Tracing: Enable traces to follow specific body points over time
  • Joint Angles: Toggle angle visualization for biomechanical analysis
  • Person Masks: Add body segmentation overlays
  • Hand Tracking: Enable detailed finger and hand movement tracking

Performance Tips

  • Use consistent, good lighting for best pose detection
  • Avoid busy backgrounds that might interfere with detection
  • Close other apps for optimal performance during recording
  • Keep your device charged during longer recording sessions

Privacy & Data

All pose detection and video processing happens entirely on your device. Your movement data never leaves your iPhone/iPad. For complete details, see our Privacy Policy.
